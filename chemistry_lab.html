CHEMISTRY LAB


1. a) Recognition of chemical substances and solutions used in the laboratory by senses & b) Familiarization of methods for Volumetric analysis

1.0 Practice volumetric measurements (using pipettes, measuring jars, volumetric flask, burettes) and gravimetric measurements (using different types of balances), making dilutions, etc To identify the chemical compounds and solutions by senses.

2. Preparation of Std Na2CO3 and making solutions of different dilution solution.

2.0 Practice making standard solutions with pre weighed salts and to make solutions of desired dilutions using appropriate techniques.

3. EstimationofHClsolutionusingStd.Na2CO3solution

3.0 Conduct titrations adopting standard procedures and using Std. Na2CO3solutionfor estimation of HCl

4. Estimation of NaOHusing Std. HCl solution

4.0 Conduct titrations adopting standard procedures and using Std.HClsolution for estimation of NaOH

5. Estimation of H2SO4usingStd.NaOH solution

5.0 Conduct titrations adopting standard procedures and using Std. NaOH solution for estimation of H2SO4

6. Estimation of Mohr’s Salt usingStd.KMnO4

6.0 Conduct titrations adopting standard procedures and using Std.KMnO4solution for estimation of Mohr’s Salt

7. Determination of acidity of water sample

7.0 Conduct titrations adopting standard procedures to determine the acidity of given samples of water (One ground water and one surface / tap water, and rain water if available)

8. Determination of alkalinity of water sample

8.0 Conduct titrations adopting standard procedures to determine the alkalinity of given samples of water (One ground water and one surface / tap water)

9. Determination of total hardness of water using Std. EDTA solution

9.0 Conduct titrations adopting standard procedures to determine the total hardness of given samples of water (One ground water and one surface / tap water) using Std. EDTA solution

10. Estimation of Chlorides present in water sample

10.0 Conduct titrations adopting standard procedures to determine the chlorides present in the given samples of water and wastewater (One ground water and one surface / tap water)

11. Estimation of Dissolved Oxygen(D.O)in water sample

11.0 Conduct the test using titrometric / electrometric method to determine Dissolved Oxygen (D.O) in given water samples (One sample from closed container and one from open container / tap water)

12. Determination of pH using pHmeter

12.0 Conduct the test on given samples of water / solutions (like soft drinks, sewage, etc.) to determine their pH using standard pH meter

13. Determination of conductivity of water and adjusting ionic strength required level.

13.0 Conduct the test on given samples of water / solutions

a) To determine conductivity

b) To adjust the ionic strength of the sample to the desired value

14. Determination of turbidity of water

14.0 Conduct the test on given samples of solutions (coloured and non coloured) to determine their turbidity in NTU

15. Estimation of total solids present in water sample

15.0 To determine the total solids present in given samples of water (One ground water and one surface / tap water)
